Boredom

Do you remember when you could create a world,
Where no one could enter, your secrets unfurled?
Do you remember when silence alone
Filled the mind with ideas, a laughter well-known,
That laughter, so pure, one of a kind,
Echoing softly in the heart and the mind.
Oh, I reminisce on the days forever missed,
When boredom had no place, it didn't exist.
My mind, so endless, soared high above,
Imagination took the crown, and kingdoms were loved.
Oh, how I reminisce on those days,
Where every thought would set fire to the maze.
Do you remember when you could relieve that feeling?
The feeling of nothing to do, so appealing.
Do you remember when all that it took
Was a mystery to solve, a forgotten book?
But now, it feels like I'm missing the clue,
The magic of moments that once came through.

Petals & Static

everything I know about love,
full of untold secrets.
bell jar, unraveled-
a woman’s mind, her rationale.
bunny, I love you,
bunny, you’re sooo cute.
a woman’s world.
holding the intrinsic lust of intellect.
in a woman’s world they are fierce-
with metallic spikes
and bubblegum bows.
a singular petal drifting,
its beauty known
through every sense.
for she is the one
who dances in the wind.
a woman’s world.
like novels you fall inside,
where soul and story intertwine.
orchids bloom again and again-
so too shall she cradle you,
year after year.
a woman’s world:
soft but hard.
for softness is truth,
and strength wears a gloss.
synesthesia surging,
it’s just as they are:
feeling, seeing,
living through scars.
we giggle, we gossip,
we carry the unseen-
something sacred,
something vital,
something bright beneath the lace.
not built to be understood,
only to endure in elegance.
in a woman’s world
we flow with the tides,
we sync with the moon,
we are our own brides.
a sacred bond with one another-
language passed in glances,
half-remembered lullabies.
mirror-slick and ever-shifting,
like silk over bone.
she is not yours to know,
only to notice
in the hush between moments,
in the static
before the song plays.

Barefoot

The grass cools against my soles
as I step past the warning of my mother’s voice,
the one that says, Don’t go barefoot,
but I do anyway-
the blades tickling my arches,
each step an act of rebellion,
soft underfoot until the earth begins to hum,
and the cicadas join in,
their song rising like a heat wave,
filling the evening with a pulse,
thrum, thrum, thrum.
The sky is a velvet stage for fireworks,
the air heavy with salt and smoke.
We run-
feet slapping,
bare against the grass,
heartbeats matching the bursts above.
I feel the burn in my legs,
the ache of freedom,
then-
Ouch!
A rock underfoot, sharp and unforgiving,
but I climb anyway-
how could I resist?
The tide pools,
those little worlds trapped in time,
they whisper secrets-
look here,
find a treasure,
so I become the detective.
The beach tugs at me like a magnet,
sand slipping between my toes,
hotter than I imagined,
but I don’t care-
I’m too busy
watching the ocean swallow the sun,
my feet sinking deeper into the earth,
chasing the sound of the ice cream truck.
The bell rings in the distance,
sweet and familiar,
a tune that means more than just sugar-
it means everything.
I bite off the layer of chocolate crunch,
cracking it with my teeth
before licking the ice cream,
cool and sweet,
melting faster than I can catch it.
I hear the crickets now,
their chorus a lullaby for the end of the day,
and then-
the fireflies.
Tiny stars flicker in the dark,
catching my breath
like something forgotten-
when was the last time
I saw a firefly?
The salty water clings to my skin,
the sun already starting to evaporate
my wet body,
leaving me sticky
as the sand coats me,
a second skin I can’t shake.
My mother’s voice again,
this time more gentle-
a rinse,
a shower,
a quick scrub before the ride home.
But I am still barefoot in my story,
the world still alive beneath my feet-
no matter how much they tell me to put shoes on,
Barefoot, I keep summer in my body-
its grit in my skin,
its sugar on my tongue,
and sometimes, when the world goes quiet,
I can still hear it humming underfoot.
